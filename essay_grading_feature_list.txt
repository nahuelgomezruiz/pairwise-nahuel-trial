Essay Grading Feature List — Components and Required Ingredients
=================================================================

Legend
- [Rule-based]: computed with counting or simple rules; no trained model required
- [Needs prompt]: requires the actual assignment prompt text
- [Needs sources]: requires the source texts provided to students
- [Requires model]: requires a trained language model to judge meaning

Global normalization
- For every count, also compute a rate per 100 words.
- Keep total word count as a separate feature.
- Normalize quotation marks and spacing before counting.

Common ingredients (shared resources you will likely need)
- essay_text: the student’s full essay as plain text
- prompt_text: the assignment prompt, if available
- source_texts: the source passages given to students, if available
- english_dictionary: a word list for spelling checks
- frequency_list: word frequencies from a general corpus
- academic_word_list: a published list of academic vocabulary
- stopword_list: common function words to ignore when measuring content overlap
- transition_phrase_list: “however”, “therefore”, “on the other hand”, etc.
- reporting_verb_list: “states”, “argues”, “claims”, “suggests”, etc.
- vague_term_list: “things”, “stuff”, “a lot”, “very”, “really”, etc.
- hedging_word_list: “might”, “may”, “perhaps”, “possibly”, etc.
- certainty_word_list: “obviously”, “clearly”, “undeniably”, etc.
- cliche_list: “in today’s society”, “since the dawn of time”, etc.
- author_title_list: author names and titles of the sources (if provided)
- syllable_counter: for readability formulas

=================================================================
A) Length and basic counts  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
1) Total words
   Ingredients: essay_text

2) Total characters (with and without spaces)
   Ingredients: essay_text

3) Sentence count
   Ingredients: essay_text (after reliable sentence splitting)

4) Paragraph count
   Ingredients: essay_text (after reliable paragraph splitting)

5) Average sentence length (in words)
   Ingredients: essay_text

6) Median sentence length (in words)
   Ingredients: essay_text

7) Share of very short sentences (for example, under 10 words)
   Ingredients: essay_text

8) Share of very long sentences (for example, over 25 words)
   Ingredients: essay_text

9) Unique word count
   Ingredients: essay_text

10) Lexical diversity (unique-to-total word ratio)
    Ingredients: essay_text

11) Words used exactly once (count and rate)
    Ingredients: essay_text

12) Long word rate (for example, words with 7 or more characters)
    Ingredients: essay_text

=================================================================
B) Punctuation and symbol use  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
13) Commas per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

14) Semicolons per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

15) Colons per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

16) Dashes per 100 words (hyphen, en dash, em dash)
    Ingredients: essay_text

17) Parentheses and brackets per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

18) Quotation marks (opening, closing, unmatched) per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

19) Question marks per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

20) Exclamation marks per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

21) Ellipses per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

22) Apostrophes per 100 words
    Ingredients: essay_text

23) Unbalanced punctuation flags (for example, unmatched quotes or parentheses)
    Ingredients: essay_text

24) Double punctuation flags (for example, “??”, “!!”)
    Ingredients: essay_text

25) Double spaces after punctuation (rate)
    Ingredients: essay_text

=================================================================
C) Spelling and mechanics  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
26) Spelling error count and rate (dictionary-based)
    Ingredients: essay_text, english_dictionary

27) Unknown word rate (not found in dictionary)
    Ingredients: essay_text, english_dictionary

28) Capitalization errors (sentence starts and proper names where obvious)
    Ingredients: essay_text, english_dictionary (optional for proper names)

29) Quotation punctuation correctness (comma and period placement around quotes)
    Ingredients: essay_text

30) Repeated characters or words flags (for example, “sooooo”, “very very”)
    Ingredients: essay_text

31) Consistent spacing and punctuation style (straight vs smart quotes)
    Ingredients: essay_text

=================================================================
D) Sentence structure proxies  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
32) Subordinator rate (“because”, “although”, “since”, “while”, “if”, “unless”, “whereas”, “though”, “when”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, list of subordinating conjunctions

33) Coordinating conjunction rate (“for”, “and”, “nor”, “but”, “or”, “yet”, “so”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, list of coordinating conjunctions

34) Clause proxy: commas per sentence
    Ingredients: essay_text

35) Clause proxy: semicolons per sentence
    Ingredients: essay_text

36) Parallel structure markers in lists (for example, repeated grammatical patterns)
    Ingredients: essay_text (pattern rules)

=================================================================
E) Organization and coherence (rule-based proxies)  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
37) Paragraph length statistics (average and spread)
    Ingredients: essay_text

38) Single-sentence paragraph share and very long paragraph share
    Ingredients: essay_text

39) Transition phrase rate (for example, “however”, “therefore”, “in contrast”, “as a result”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, transition_phrase_list

40) Transition phrase variety (number of different transitions used)
    Ingredients: essay_text, transition_phrase_list

41) Local cohesion: content-word overlap between neighboring sentences
    Ingredients: essay_text, stopword_list

42) Paragraph-to-paragraph topic continuity (overlap of key words across paragraphs)
    Ingredients: essay_text, stopword_list

43) Redundancy and repetition (reused phrases or sentences)
    Ingredients: essay_text

=================================================================
F) Alignment with the assignment prompt (proxies)  [Needs prompt]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
44) Word and phrase overlap with the prompt (rate)
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text, stopword_list

45) Coverage of required terms listed in the prompt (for example, named items or concepts)
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text

46) Word-count within the required range from the prompt
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text

47) Required sections named in the prompt are present (for example, “Summary”, “Analysis”, “Reflection” if explicitly requested)
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text

=================================================================
G) Use of sources and evidence (proxies)  [Rule-based; some need sources]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
48) Direct quotation count and quoted words as a percentage of the essay
    Ingredients: essay_text

49) Average quote length
    Ingredients: essay_text

50) Attribution phrase rate near evidence (“according to”, “the author”, “the article”, “the passage”, “states”, “argues”, “writes”, “reports”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, reporting_verb_list

51) Number of distinct source mentions by name or title (string match)
    Ingredients: essay_text, author_title_list  [Needs sources to build the list]

52) Balance across sources (no single source dominates) using counts by source name
    Ingredients: essay_text, author_title_list  [Needs sources]

53) Quote proportion bands (very low, moderate, very high) to flag over-reliance on quoting
    Ingredients: essay_text

54) Matching quoted spans against the source texts to verify exactness (string match with fuzziness)
    Ingredients: essay_text, source_texts  [Needs sources]

55) Overlap of short word sequences with sources to flag patchwriting
    Ingredients: essay_text, source_texts, stopword_list  [Needs sources]

=================================================================
H) Reasoning and argument (proxies)  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
56) Counterargument signal phrases (“some may argue”, “critics contend”, “on the other hand”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, phrase list

57) Refutation signal phrases (“however”, “yet”, “nevertheless”, “this fails to account for”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, phrase list

58) Reason and explanation markers (“because”, “therefore”, “as a result”, “this shows that”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, phrase list

59) Consistency of stance markers (rate of contradiction phrases like “but” + reversal within the same paragraph)
    Ingredients: essay_text

=================================================================
I) Vocabulary and style  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
60) Rare word rate (based on general frequency list)
    Ingredients: essay_text, frequency_list

61) Academic vocabulary rate (match against a published academic word list)
    Ingredients: essay_text, academic_word_list

62) Vagueness rate (matches from vague_term_list)
    Ingredients: essay_text, vague_term_list

63) Hedging rate (matches from hedging_word_list)
    Ingredients: essay_text, hedging_word_list

64) Over-certainty rate (matches from certainty_word_list)
    Ingredients: essay_text, certainty_word_list

65) Cliché rate (matches from cliche_list)
    Ingredients: essay_text, cliche_list

66) Contraction rate and slang rate
    Ingredients: essay_text, slang list (optional)

67) Uppercase ratio and digit ratio
    Ingredients: essay_text

=================================================================
J) Readability and fluency  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
68) Flesch Reading Ease score
    Ingredients: essay_text, syllable_counter

69) Flesch–Kincaid Grade Level
    Ingredients: essay_text, syllable_counter

70) Coleman–Liau index
    Ingredients: essay_text

71) Gunning fog index
    Ingredients: essay_text, syllable_counter

72) Dale–Chall score
    Ingredients: essay_text, simple-word list for Dale–Chall

73) Variation in sentence length (spread)
    Ingredients: essay_text

=================================================================
K) Cohesion devices and discourse signals  [Rule-based]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
74) Count of examples, definitions, statistics, and quotations labeled with signal words
    Ingredients: essay_text, phrase lists

75) Metadiscourse signals (“this essay shows”, “the following section”, “in this paragraph”)
    Ingredients: essay_text, phrase list

76) Lexical cohesion: repetition and rephrasing of key ideas across paragraphs (measured by shared content words)
    Ingredients: essay_text, stopword_list

=================================================================
L) Formatting and presentation  [Rule-based; some need prompt]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
77) Title present and relevant (non-empty, not generic filler)
    Ingredients: essay_text

78) Paragraphing present and consistent (line breaks or indent markers)
    Ingredients: essay_text

79) Consistent punctuation style (straight vs smart quotes, dashes)
    Ingredients: essay_text

80) Citation list or “Works Cited” present if the prompt requires it
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text  [Needs prompt]

81) In-text citation names match entries in the reference list
    Ingredients: essay_text, author_title_list  [Needs sources]

=================================================================
M) Integrity checks  [Rule-based; some need sources]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
82) Duplicate or near-duplicate passages inside the essay (self-duplication)
    Ingredients: essay_text

83) Overlap of short word sequences with source texts
    Ingredients: essay_text, source_texts, stopword_list  [Needs sources]

84) Overlap with other essays in the set to flag potential sharing
    Ingredients: essay_text, other essays’ texts

=================================================================
N) Task-specific compliance  [Rule-based; needs prompt and sources when applicable]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
85) Word count within the range required by the prompt
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text  [Needs prompt]

86) Number of sources used meets the minimum required by the prompt
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text, author_title_list  [Needs prompt and sources]

87) Required named sections from the prompt are present
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text  [Needs prompt]

=================================================================
O) Minimal model-based components (essential only)  [Requires model]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
88) Prompt adherence (topic and task fit)
    Outputs:
      - Average similarity between essay sentences and the prompt
      - Share of sentences that align with the prompt (coverage)
      - Off-topic rate (sentences below a similarity floor)
      - Task-match flag (argument, explanation, comparison, as requested)
    Ingredients: essay_text, prompt_text
    Method: sentence-level similarity to the prompt and a small classifier for task type

89) Evidence grounding against the source texts
    Outputs:
      - Supported-claim rate (claims linked to at least one source passage)
      - Contradiction rate
      - Misattribution count (wrong source credited)
      - Paraphrase-versus-quote support rate
    Ingredients: essay_text, source_texts, author_title_list
    Method: passage-level similarity and entailment-style checks between essay claims and source passages

90) Thesis (central claim) detection
    Outputs:
      - Thesis present (0 or 1)
      - Thesis position (from start to end of the essay)
      - Thesis specificity score (generic versus specific wording)
    Ingredients: essay_text
    Method: classifier that finds a single central claim sentence and rates specificity

91) Evidence–explanation linkage
    Outputs:
      - Explained-evidence rate (evidence followed by explanation that ties it to a claim)
      - Orphan-quote rate (evidence with no follow-up explanation)
      - Counterargument-and-refutation present (0 or 1)
    Ingredients: essay_text
    Method: sequence classifier to detect “introduce evidence → explain → connect to claim” patterns

=================================================================
Notes on implementation
- Compute both raw counts and rates per 100 words for all countable items.
- For features that “need the prompt,” include them only when the prompt is available.
- For features that “need sources,” include them only when the source texts or at least author names are available.
- Keep all model-based components to the four essentials listed above.
